Ẏ/Y — War vs Epidemic
Output growth comparison
War (K destroyed, ω < ω*):
z rises above z* → high return to capital → investment floods in → K rebuilt fast. Consumption growth is high immediately. u > u* — people work more than they study. Recovery is rapid because K-scarcity raises the return to capital automatically.
Epidemic (H destroyed, ω > ω*):
z falls below z* → low return to capital → weak investment incentive. Education sector needs H to produce H — scarce factor is also the input to its own replenishment. u < u* — more study, less work — but rebuilding is slow. Self-reinforcing drag.
